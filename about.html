About Me

Curious about growth and impact, I transitioned from project coordination to product management, driven by continuous learning and design.

I’m Priyanka!

I started my career as a Project Coordinator, where I quickly became curious about making a greater impact with my skills. This curiosity led me to the world of Product Management. Determined to excel, I pursued online courses, including Google Project Management, Google UX/UI, Meta Frontend, IBM Product Management, and Maryland University's Product Management course. Each course added new depth to my understanding, fueling my ambition to become a Product Manager who can guide meaningful, user-centered products.

Alongside my journey in Product Management, I’ve developed a strong passion for Product Design. At Ransh Innovations Pvt Ltd, I had the opportunity to design a web app for both the admin and user sides of a blogging platform, marking my first project in web app design. I soon took on the challenge of designing the MVP for Plunge, a social networking app, where I led the design process as the sole designer. Creating Plunge’s design system was a particularly enriching experience, as I relied on insights from UI Design Systems Mastery by Marina Budarina to develop a cohesive, efficient system for the MVP.

My reading habit has been instrumental in building deeper insights and learning with intent. It’s given me the advantage of approaching each project with a fresh perspective, enabling me to quickly grasp and apply complex design principles. As I handled every aspect of Plunge's design journey—from crafting PRDs and conducting user research to ideating workflows—this habit of continuous learning helped me refine my skills, bridging my interests in both design and product management.

My time at Limurse further sharpened my strategic thinking, as I led market research, competitive analysis, and product-market fit initiatives to support app growth. Developing a business model and finding early adopters offered me a holistic view of product positioning in competitive markets. With each project, I’m excited to blend my skills and insights, moving closer to my goal of becoming a Product Manager who builds impactful, innovative products for users.

I’d love to discuss how I can contribute to a dynamic team and bring user-centered innovation to life.
